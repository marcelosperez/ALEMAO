Superlative and Comparative
https://www.fluentu.com/blog/german/comparative-and-superlative-german/#:~:text=In%20German%2C%20comparatives%20are%20often,of%20an%20adjective%20or%20adverb.


Comparatives are often formed by adding +er to the end of an adjective or adverb (just like in English).

+er als (als=than)

(Nominative)
Ich bin schneller als du. (I am faster than you.)
Er ist größer als du. (He is taller than you.)

(Accusative)
Mama mag mich besser als dich. (Mom likes me better than you.)



Superlatives are formed by appending +sten or +esten to the end of an adjective or adverb.

am ...+sten +ßten
am ...+esten

(Feminine)
Meine ist die vollste Flasche. (Mine is the fullest bottle.)
Meine Flasche ist am vollsten. (My bottle is the fullest.)

(Masculine)
Ich bin der jüngste Bruder. (I am the youngest brother.)
Ich bin am jüngsten. (I am youngest.)






Not comparative. schneller (faster) has that -er on the end simply because Freund (friend) is masculine. In this case, it’s not comparative. It’s just descriptive. You’re talking about your fast friend.

mein schneller Freund (my fast friend)


Yes, the comparative and masculine look alike. In this case, schneller (faster) has that -er on the end again, but it’s for a different reason. In this case, it’s because we’re using.

mein schneller Freund (my faster friend)








Exceptions to the Formula. When you’re using a monosyllabic adjective, sometimes you may need to toss an umlaut too.

laut (loud), lauter (louder), am lautesten (the loudest)

groß (big), größer (bigger), am größten (the biggest)

alt (old), älter (older), am ältesten (the oldest)

jung (young), jünger (younger), am jüngsten (the youngest)

viel (much), mehr (more), am meisten (the most)

gut (good), besser (better), am besten (the best)

gern (like/with pleasure), lieber (love to/prefer to), am liebsten (the favorite/with the most pleasure)

hoch (tall), höher (taller), am höchsten (the tallest)





Comparative of equality

Ich bin so schnell wie du. (I am as fast as you.)
